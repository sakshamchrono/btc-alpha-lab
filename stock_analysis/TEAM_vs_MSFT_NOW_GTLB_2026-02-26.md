# In-Depth Fundamental + Technical Comparison
## Atlassian (TEAM) vs Microsoft (MSFT) vs ServiceNow (NOW) vs GitLab (GTLB)

Date: 2026-02-26 (UTC)

> **Important:** This is research, not investment advice. Data is compiled from public web sources and should be cross-checked against official filings (10-Q/10-K, investor relations decks, earnings transcripts) before deploying capital.

---

## Executive Summary (Bottom Line)

- **Best quality compounder:** **MSFT** (strongest profitability, broad moat, AI capex scale, still premium valuation).
- **Best enterprise workflow execution:** **NOW** (excellent subscription quality + margins, but premium and sentiment-sensitive).
- **Highest upside but higher uncertainty:** **TEAM** and **GTLB** (strong growth + improving margins, but weaker technical structure and competitive pressure).
- **If choosing one for risk-adjusted conviction right now:** **MSFT > NOW > TEAM ≈ GTLB**.
- **If specifically evaluating TEAM as a buy now:** Better as a **staggered accumulation** candidate, not an all-in momentum buy, until technical trend repair confirms.

## 1) Fundamental Comparison

## 1.1 Latest business momentum (earnings/guidance snapshot)

### Atlassian (TEAM)
- Q2 FY26 revenue: **~$1.59B**, about **+23% YoY**.
- Cloud revenue: **~$1.1B**, about **+26% YoY**.
- Non-GAAP operating margin: **~27.5%**.
- Guidance (FY26): revenue growth around **~22%**.
- Key signal: strong cloud execution, but GAAP profitability still weak/negative in many snapshots.

### Microsoft (MSFT)
- Q2 FY26 revenue: **~$81.3B**, about **+17% YoY**.
- Azure growth: **~39% YoY** (slight sequential moderation).
- Operating margin: around **~47%**.
- Guidance: continued growth, but heavy AI capex keeps cloud margin pressure in focus.
- Key signal: elite scale + margin + ecosystem lock-in.

### ServiceNow (NOW)
- Q4 2025 subscription revenue: **~$3.47B**, around **+21% YoY**.
- cRPO growth: around **+25% YoY**.
- Non-GAAP operating margin: around **~31%**.
- FY26 subscription growth guidance: roughly **19.5–20% (CC)**.
- Key signal: very strong enterprise execution + renewals (reported high retention).

### GitLab (GTLB)
- Q3 FY26 revenue: **~$244M**, around **+25% YoY**.
- Non-GAAP operating margin: around **~18%** (improving).
- FY26 guidance: continued strong growth, but mixed macro pockets.
- Key signal: high-growth DevSecOps + AI feature monetization optionality.

## 1.2 Valuation posture (relative)

> Metrics vary by source/date; ranges below are directional.

- **MSFT**: Forward P/E roughly **~23x**, EV/Sales around **~9–10x**.
- **TEAM**: TTM P/E often negative; Forward P/E broad range **~16–33x**, EV/Sales around **~3–4x**.
- **GTLB**: TTM P/E often negative; Forward P/E around **~25–32x**, EV/Sales around **~3x+**.
- **NOW**: Premium SaaS-style valuation (noted high multiple profile vs legacy software, though below peak risk-on periods).

### Interpretation
- MSFT and NOW justify premium via durability/profitability.
- TEAM/GTLB can look cheaper on sales multiples, but carry execution + competition + profitability transition risk.

## 1.3 Moat + competitive positioning

### MSFT
- Deepest moat via ecosystem bundling: Azure + GitHub + M365 + security + distribution.
- AI infra scale is difficult to replicate.

### NOW
- Strong moat in enterprise workflows/ITSM and expanding AI workflow stack.
- High switching costs in large deployments.

### TEAM
- Strong in developer/project collaboration stack (Jira/Confluence/JSM).
- Key risk: competition from MSFT/GitHub, NOW, GitLab, Linear, Asana/Monday.

### GTLB
- Strong DevSecOps identity and integrated platform narrative.
- Competes directly with GitHub ecosystem gravity.

## 1.4 Recent news/catalysts (AI-focused)

- **TEAM:** expanding AI agents in Jira and broader AI workflow positioning.
- **MSFT:** aggressive AI infra expansion, Copilot ecosystem growth, partner ecosystem leverage.
- **NOW:** deepened AI partnerships (including with frontier model providers), partner program expansion.

## 4) TEAM-specific Buy Decision (vs competitors)

## Is TEAM a good buy **right now**?
**Answer:** **Conditionally yes**, but only with disciplined entry.

Why not full conviction now:
1. Technical trend still weak compared to MSFT quality profile.
2. Competitive pressure is intense (MSFT/NOW/GTLB/others).
3. Profitability optics still less robust than top-tier software peers.

Why it can still work:
1. Strong cloud growth and improving non-GAAP margins.
2. Solid product relevance in engineering/IT workflows.
3. Lower sales multiple than some quality SaaS peers can provide upside if execution sustains.

### Practical stance
- Prefer **tranche/DCA** entry over lump-sum.
- Add on trend repair confirmation (higher low + reclaim of key MAs).
- Keep risk controls tight until chart structure improves.
