## 1) Fundamental analysis

## Business and growth profile
Atlassian remains a high-quality software platform asset (Jira, Confluence, Service Management, Loom, Rovo/AI layer), but the stock has undergone extreme derating. Fundamental operating momentum is still positive:

- **FY2025 revenue:** **$5.215B**, up **19.66% YoY** (StockAnalysis financials)
- **TTM revenue:** **$5.76B**, up **20.13% YoY** (StockAnalysis financials/statistics)
- **Q2 FY26 shareholder letter highlights (company source):**
  - Revenue **$1.6B**, **+23% YoY**
  - Cloud revenue **$1.1B**, **+26% YoY**
  - RPO **$3.8B**, **+44% YoY**
  - Cloud NRR **120%+**

Interpretation: core topline + enterprise adoption signal remains strong; this is not a classic demand-collapse profile.

## Profitability and margins
Atlassian still shows a GAAP profitability gap, but strong cash conversion:

- **Gross margin (TTM): 84.06%**
- **Operating margin (TTM): -2.25%**
- **Net margin (TTM): -3.29%**
- **FCF margin (TTM): 22.25%**
- **FCF (TTM): $1.282B**

View: margins reflect ongoing product/platform investments (including AI and enterprise go-to-market), while FCF profile indicates underlying economic strength.

## Balance sheet quality
From StockAnalysis statistics:

- **Cash & equivalents:** $1.57B
- **Total debt:** $1.22B
- **Net cash:** **+$351M**
- **Current ratio:** 0.89
- **Debt/Equity:** 0.76

Balance sheet is not distressed; net cash plus strong FCF materially lowers solvency risk versus highly levered software names.

## Valuation and peer context
TEAM appears inexpensive versus its own multi-year history and many software peers on sales-based multiples:

- **TEAM P/S (TTM): ~3.35** (StockAnalysis statistics; CompaniesMarketCap also reports ~3.54 as of Feb 2026)
- **TEAM EV/Sales: ~3.29**
- **TEAM Forward P/E: ~14.44** (not always stable due GAAP loss optics / estimate sensitivity)

Peer P/S snapshots (CompaniesMarketCap, Feb 2026):
- **ServiceNow (NOW): 8.28**
- **Salesforce (CRM): 4.53**
- **Snowflake (SNOW): 13.2**

Conclusion on valuation: TEAM trades at a deep discount to premium growth software despite still-healthy growth and FCF generation. Key caveat: market may be pricing in sustained deceleration + margin uncertainty.

## Framework used

1. Revenue path (FY26/FY27 run-rate implied by recent growth),
2. Exit valuation multiple (P/S / EV/S range),
3. Risk-adjusted probability weighting.

Given current market regime, we use conservative multiple assumptions vs historical software peaks.

### Scenario assumptions

### Bear case (25% probability)
- Revenue growth slows materially (mid-teens to low-teens trajectory)
- Multiple stays compressed (~2.8x sales)
- Sentiment remains risk-off toward unprofitable-GAAP software
- **Target: $58**
